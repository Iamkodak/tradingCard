Axolotl

An Axolotl is a paedomorphic salamander. Its is unusual among amphibians in that it reached adulthood without undergoing metarmorphosis. Instaed of taking to the land, adults remain aquatic and gilled. The species was originally found in several lakes underlying what is now mexico city, such as lake Xochimilco, and lake Chalco.This lake was drained by the spanish settlers after the conquests of the Aztec Empire, leading to the destruction of much of the axolotl's natural habitat.

Asexually matured aduly axolotl, at ade 18-27 months, range in length from 15-45cm, although a size close to 23cm is most common and greater than 30cm is rare. Axolotls possess features typical of salamander larvae, including external gills and a caudal fin extending from behind the head to the vent. External gills are usually lost when salamander species mature into adulthood, although the axolotl maintains this feature. This is due to their neoteny evoloution, where axolotls are much aquatic than the other salamder species. Males are identified by thier swollen cloacae lined with papilae, while females are noticeable for their wider bodies full of eggs.
